## Why Build Your Own?

Stock keyboards are fine. But once you've typed on a well-tuned custom board, going back feels like driving a rental car after owning a sports car.

## Choosing a PCB

I went with the **Bakeneko65** — an open-source 65% layout with a gasket mount. The gasket absorbs typing vibration and gives a distinctly bouncy feel.

## Switches and Lubing

I chose Gateron Yellow linears. Lubing with Krytox 205g0 took about 4 hours for 70 switches but the silky result was worth every minute.

## QMK Firmware

QMK is open-source keyboard firmware with massive customization — layers, tap-dance, macros, RGB. Flash it via `qmk flash` after editing your keymap JSON.

## Final Thoughts

The process taught me PCB soldering, firmware basics, and a lot about acoustics. My next build will be an Alice layout with brass weight.
