## Why Plugins Matter

Out of the box, Zsh is already excellent. Plugins push it into another tier — autocomplete that actually works, syntax highlighting mid-command, smarter history search.

## The Essential Five

**zsh-autosuggestions** — shows ghost completions from history as you type. Accept with →. Saves hundreds of keystrokes daily.

**zsh-syntax-highlighting** — colors commands green when valid, red when not. Catches typos before you hit Enter.

**fzf-tab** — replaces the default tab completion with a fuzzy finder. Transformative.

**z (zoxide)** — smarter `cd` that learns your most-visited directories. Jump anywhere with 2-3 characters.

**you-should-use** — gently reminds you when an alias exists for what you just typed. Builds muscle memory fast.

## Plugin Manager: Zinit

I use Zinit for lazy loading and fast startup. My shell loads in under 100ms with 12 plugins enabled.
